Genoa


Genoa is a port city and the capital of the Liguria region in northwest Italy. It's known for its central role in maritime trade over many centuries. It's been a host to massive shipyards since the 19th century. It's also been a major financial center since 1407.

Genoa is believed to be the birthplace of Christopher Columbus. There's even a house he's rumored to have lived in. However, the house is an 18th century recreation. The orginal was destroyed in 1684 by a naval bombing by the French.

Before the invention of trains, planes, or cars, sailing was the main way to get to the outside world. Genoa had multiple shipyards built for this reason. During World War I, ships would dock there. During World War II, it suffered heavy attacks from bombs.
